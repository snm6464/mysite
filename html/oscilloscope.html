★ the idea ★

  • a digital oscilloscope
  • connect 4 astable multivibrator oscillators (ASMVs)
  • mix all 4 into one signal
  • create a visualization on p5.js

★ sketches ★

★ the process ★

  1. created 4 ASMVs
  2. wired each ASMV to one row on the breadboard
  3. experimented with resistors to vary the signal coming from each
  4. decided to use potentiometers to mix
  5. wired to one analog pin on an Adafruit Metro
  6. created code to read the data coming in
  7. plotted points on p5.js

★ taking it further ★

  • i wanted a more physical representation of this data - what does it mean?
  • i drew inspiration from vibrating plates with sand on top
  • i hooked up a transducer and attached it to a plate
  • i poured salt on top and observed its behavior

★ final product ★

★ reflections ★

The salt's movement on the plate did not work the way I thought it would. However, I still thoroughly enjoyed the process of this project. The oscilloscope itself turned out beautifully, and I really enjoyed being able to mix and hear the transducer change pitch with each new frequency. If I had more time, I would have done more research on how to make formations with vibrations, and tweaked the oscillators from there.
