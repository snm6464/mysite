★ the idea ★

  • lean into my passion for textile arts
  • create a rug out of fragments - fragmented photos, fragmented textiles
  • connect with my Persian lineage on my dad's side - the more a rug is used, the more 'valuable' it is
  • integrate family photos, scrap fabric, and my own textile work
  • when the user steps on the rug, audio will be triggered related to my relationship to my family
  • originally, the steps would trigger movements of elements on a rug projection on the wall
  • transfer photos onto fabric, make pessure sensors, edit audios

★ the process ★

pressure plates

  1. i originally wanted to measure the pressure plate values with a sensor that was more like a variable resistor
  2. i made a paper prototype of a sensor with a voltage divider and velostat in the middle
  3. i realized it would be difficult to measure a universal on/off state with this method, which is what i wanted
  4. i decided to create more of a button-like sensor with copper tape and foam in the middle
  5. i tested out cutting holes in the foam and made sure an on/off state could be achieved
  6. i created the final form of the sensor: a fabric sleeve with foam in the middle, lined with copper tape for flexibility, conductivity, and resilience

audio

  1. i knew i wanted to trigger some sort of nostalgic memory with the interactions on the rug
  2. i was able to find a series of Zoom calls done with my deceased grandfather by my father
  3. i also scavenged for some home video from my childhood that i could pull audio from
  4. i reviewed the footage i collected and clipped a few seconds from moments i wanted to capture
  5. i added reverb and other echo effects using Reaper and then exported the files
  6. i loaded the files into a processing file and created different arrays with the audio

the rug

  1. i purchased various fabric scraps and natural fibers from FabScrap
  2. i gathered old photos of my family members from Tehran in the 60s and scanned them
  3. i printed the images using a laser printer and transferred them onto fabric
  4. i wove several pieces that either framed the images or were pieces of their own
  5. i cut out the transferred photos and attached them to the woven pieces using superglue (as a temporary fix)
  6. i gathered other fabric scraps and arranged all of the pieces in a formation i liked
  7. i glued all the pieces together and then sewed them together using gold thread
  8. i created "fragments" of the rug - pieces that would extend from the main piece and could be stepped on

interconnection

  1. pressure plates are stepped on and send a signal to the microcontroller (Adafruit Metro Mini)
  2. code in the Arduino IDE interprets the incoming values and prints an array of values (0 or 1) to the Serial port
  3. code in Processing then interprets whether or not a sound is already playing and if a sound needs to be triggered
  4. using the sound library, Processing plays a sound out of the two computer speakers
  5. the computer is connected to two speakers that play the sound

★ reflections ★

I’m incredibly happy with how this piece turned out. It worked exactly how I wanted to and, while it took a slightly different form than what I had envisioned, I still feel as though the piece represented myself and my journey in the way I intended it to. It was incredibly fulfilling to see that people not only enjoyed interacting with the piece, but many people actually immersed themselves in the piece by sitting in the furniture and truly taking it in. I am also incredibly satisfied with the fact that the piece evoked emotion and memories for many onlookers. I would prbably have sourced more material to recreate a living room setting. It would have been nice to have a lamp or other types of lighting to create more of an ambiance. Additionally, I’d have made more pieces, possibly even more textile pieces that could have been used on the walls. I don’t think I’d change what I created, however. I envision this project being a possible thesis exploration with more time to explore textile types, weaving techniques, etc. I hope to make this a greater, larger project in the future and explore my relationship to my culture as I grow and develop as an adult!
