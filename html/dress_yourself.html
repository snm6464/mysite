★ the idea ★

  • a "dressable" lamp with different shades depending on the intended mood of the user
  • three garment lampshades
  • a lasercut hanger and three lasercut hangers made out of birch wood sheets
  • blue for energy, red for relaxation, and yellow for warmth

★ sketches ★

★ the process ★

the stand

  1. made a paper stand prototype
  2. created laser cut assets with Adobe Illustrator
  3. laser cut the pieces on a slab of birch wood
  4. stained each piece of the stand and each hanger
  5. wood glued the pieces of the stand together

the garments

  1. made a paper pattern for a short sleeve and a long sleeve
  2. bought scrap fabric
  3. used a stamp i made with red ink
  4. sewed the pieces together with a sewing machine
  5. added embellishments with beads and ribbons

the closet

  1. cut each piece of birch wood
  2. glued the pieces together to make a cube
  3. stained each side of the closet piece
  4. added ribbon embellishments and a closet rod

the book

  1. gathered scrap fabrics and paper
  2. glued each piece of fabric to the paper
  3. named each piece and designed for aesthetic continuity

★ final product ★

★ reflections ★

This final project was eye-opening in how to go about completing a larger-scale piece of work, with it being my first. It was interesting to navigate breaking it up into small pieces in order to complete it, and it was very satisfying to see the completed piece come to fruition as I completed more and more of the steps. It was the most satisfying to have a completed piece and see what I’d accomplished. If I had more time, I would have made the light brighter and possibly done the lighting myself. In the gallery, I do not feel as though my needs were met when it came to my room placement and setup, but I am still very happy with my work and with the feedback from the critics.
